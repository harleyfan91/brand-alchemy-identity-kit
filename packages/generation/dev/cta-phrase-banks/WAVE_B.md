## Wave C — Audience_growth creative framework

The structural problem in existing `audience_growth` copy: virtually every leaf uses the same two-sentence formula — `[Follow / subscribe for X, Y, Z]` + `[frequency promise]`. At depth this reads as generator output. The fix is not more pairs in the same shape — it's identifying distinct structural shapes per industry and distributing pairs across them so the pool reads as a diverse body of copy.

---

### Structural shapes — `trades_home` audience_growth

| Shape | Hook | Sample anchor |
|---|---|---|
| **Before/after** | Follow to see the transformation | "See what a good job looks like before you hire anyone" |
| **Local credibility** | Follow for work happening in your neighborhood | "Next post might be a job on your street" |
| **Pre-need tip** | Position as useful before the reader has a job | "Follow before you need us" |
| **Seasonal trigger** | Time the follow to the season they'll need this work | "Want a heads-up before peak season" |
| **Process transparency** | Show honest job-site reality | "The real photos and the honest story" |

Every `trades_home` audience_growth leaf must use at least three shapes.

---

### Structural shapes — `food_hospitality` audience_growth

| Shape | Hook | Sample anchor |
|---|---|---|
| **"What's on" access** | Follow to know the menu before deciding where to eat | "Post the daily menu every morning" |
| **Kitchen/sourcing** | Follow to see the people and sourcing behind the food | "Where it comes from, who makes it" |
| **Event/seasonal** | Follow to be first to know about events and seasonal dishes | "First to know, first to reserve" |
| **Regular connection** | Follow to stay close to a place between visits | "Stay close to the kitchen" |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | Follow for what doesn't appear on the menu | "The occasional recipe, the honest kitchen shot" |

Every `food_hospitality` audience_growth leaf must use at least four shapes.
