# WAVE E — Narrator-Tuned Variant Pools

Per §4C of the audit: the bank gains a `narrator` axis on 6 industry × narrator pairs where divergence from the industry default is meaningful. The fix is **different content**, not pronoun substitution — a solo cook and a team restaurant need different words entirely, not just `I` swapped for `we`.

Each section covers WEBSITE + EMAIL + Social Casual × `direct_sales` + `lead_gen` × available tones (3 for WEBSITE/EMAIL, 2 for Social Casual). 5 pairs per leaf. These are **new leaves**, not additions to the industry-default leaves.

---

## Narrator pairs in this wave

| Industry | Default narrator | Tuned leaf | Voice shift |
|---|---|---|---|
| `trades_home` | `solo_expert` | **`local_team`** | Crew dispatch and coverage → organized reliability replaces personal trust |
| `food_hospitality` | `local_team` | **`solo_maker`** | "I cook everything" → personal craft replaces team restaurant voice |
| `retail_maker` | `solo_maker` | **`product_led`** | Brand + collection voice → replaces single artisan "I make" |
| `health_wellness` | `solo_expert` | **`local_team`** | Multi-practitioner matching → organized breadth replaces personal expertise |
| `creative_pro` | `local_team` | **`solo_expert`** | Single creator with limited slots → personal selectivity replaces studio voice |
| `professional_svc` | `local_team` | **`solo_expert`** | "I practice independently" → personal accountability replaces firm voice |

**Quality bar:** each pair must pass the read-aloud test as something the specific narrator type would actually say. A pop-up chef doesn't say "our kitchen." A solo accountant doesn't say "our team will follow up."
